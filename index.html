Rehab Protocol

Glute Med &
Outer Hip Recovery

Targeting gluteus medius tendinopathy and deep hip rotator tightness. Built around progressive tendon loading — not rest.

Duration ~15 min
Frequency Daily
Equipment None / Band
Isometrics — calm the tendon
1
Isometric Hip Abduction (Wall)
3 × 30–45 sec hold each side  ·  rest 30 sec
Isometric
Why: Isometrics reduce tendon pain without compressive load. Start every session here before any dynamic work.
2
Isometric Glute Bridge Hold
3 × 30 sec hold  ·  feet hip-width
Isometric
Cue: Squeeze both glutes hard at the top. Don't let the hips sag or rotate.
Activation — fire the right muscles
3
Clamshells
3 × 15 each side  ·  slow and controlled
Activation
Cue: Don't let the pelvis rotate back. The movement is small — feel it in the outer hip, not the lower back.
4
Side-Lying Hip Abduction
3 × 12 each side  ·  2 sec up, 3 sec down
Activation
Cue: Toes pointed slightly down (internal rotation) targets glute med more directly than toes-up.
5
Single-Leg Glute Bridge
3 × 10 each side  ·  controlled tempo
Activation
Note: If the outer hip aches during this, drop back to the bilateral version until it settles.
Soft Tissue — not stretching
6
Lacrosse Ball Glute / Piriformis Release
60–90 sec per side  ·  find the spot and hold
Release
Important: This is sustained pressure, not rolling. Sit on the ball, find the tender point, breathe into it and wait for the release. Avoid the bony greater trochanter.
⚠ Avoid these stretches for now Pigeon pose, figure-four, and deep hip cross-body stretches compress the tendon insertion point and can actually slow healing. Counterintuitive but well-documented for gluteus medius tendinopathy. Save them for later.
Duration 30–45 min
Frequency 2–3× / week
Equipment Full gym
Warm-up — 5 min
1
90/90 Hip Transitions
2 min continuous, slow  ·  each side equally
Mobility
Why: Opens internal and external rotation without compressing the tendon the way pigeon does.
2
Banded Monster Walks
2 × 10 steps each direction
Activation
Cue: Stay low, keep tension in the band the whole time. This is a primer — not a workout set.
Primary Loading — glute med & hip stability
3
Cable Hip Abduction
3 × 12–15 each side  ·  3 sec eccentric
Strength
Key: The slow lowering (eccentric) phase is where tendons actually adapt. Don't rush it. Start light — this fatigues fast.
4
Lateral Band Walks
3 × 15 steps each direction
Strength
Cue: Slight forward lean at the hips. Keep feet parallel — don't let them turn out.
5
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
3 × 8–10 each side  ·  light-moderate weight
Strength
Why: Loads the hip in a functional pattern similar to running gait. Excellent for glute med and hip hinge strength together.
6
Lateral Step-Ups
3 × 10 each side  ·  bodyweight or light load
Strength
Cue: Drive through the heel of the working leg. Don't push off with the trailing foot — make the hip do the work.
Supplementary — glute & hip posterior chain
7
Hip Thrusts
3 × 10–12  ·  moderate load
Strength
Note: Bilateral is fine here. Focus on full extension and a strong glute contraction at the top — not just range of motion.
8
Goblet Squat
3 × 10–12  ·  moderate weight
Strength
Why: Builds quad and glute strength in a pattern that transfers directly to running. Goblet variation keeps the torso upright and reduces lateral hip stress vs. barbell back squat — better for this stage of rehab.
9
Copenhagen Plank (Adductor)
2 × 20–30 sec each side
Strength
Why: Hip stability requires adductor-abductor balance. Most runners are one-sided here and don't know it.
Cool-down — 5 min
10
Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
2 × 60 sec each side
Mobility
Safe: This doesn't compress the tendon insertion. Good post-session stretch for runners.
11
Lacrosse Ball Glute Release
60–90 sec per side
Release
Finish here — sustained pressure on any spots that feel tight after the session.
Return to running guidance Don't return to running until you can do this full gym routine pain-free for 2 consecutive sessions. Then start with walk/run intervals (e.g. 1 min run / 2 min walk × 10) rather than straight running. Build total running time by no more than 10% per week.
